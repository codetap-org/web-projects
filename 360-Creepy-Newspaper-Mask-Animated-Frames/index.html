'HALLOWEEN HOSTESS' TERROR ENDS

Notorious Beauty Found Dead in Kitchen; City Breathes Sigh of Relief on All Hallows' Eve

CODEPEN CITY, Oct. 31 — The three-year reign of terror attributed to the so-called "Halloween Hostess" came to a grim and sudden end this morning, as police discovered the body of the suspected slayer, Evelyn Harlow, on the floor of her own kitchen. Harlow, 28, whose shocking beauty and alleged depravity have held this city in a grip of fear every October, was found dead at approximately 7:30 a.m. by officers responding to a welfare check. Police found Harlow, clad in a floral house dress and a string of pearls, lying supine on the checkered linoleum of her otherwise tidy home on Elmview Avenue. Authorities noted several empty green bottles scattered on the floor near her body, though a definitive cause of death is pending a full report from the coroner's office. "It's a grim denouement to a dark chapter for Codepen City," stated a weary Police Chief Miles Brody, who was on the scene. "While the investigation is in its earliest stages, the city can rest easier tonight knowing Mrs. Harlow will not be hosting any more of her... gatherings." That chilling moniker was earned after a series of gruesome slayings that paralyzed the city. For the past three autumns, unsuspecting gentlemen, lured by Harlow's considerable charm, would vanish just before Halloween. Their bodies were discovered days later, posed in macabre, theatrical tableaus resembling ghoulish holiday decorations in various abandoned parts of the city. The constabulary had been baffled, unable to connect the poised, elegant Evelyn Harlow—a woman known for her prize-winning roses and quiet demeanor—to the string of fiendish murders. "She was always so... put together," said Mrs. Agnes Pinter, a neighbor who had alerted the police after noticing Harlow's morning papers piling up. "She'd borrow a cup of sugar! To think... to think that was living right across the street... it's just dreadful. And to be found this way, on Halloween of all nights! It's like something out of a pulp novel." To her neighbors, Evelyn Harlow was a charming, if reclusive, widow. But to the few detectives who suspected her, she was a cunning and twisted mind, a "black widow" who turned the wholesome traditions of autumn into a stage for her grotesque art. Detectives are now meticulously tearing the seemingly quaint home apart, searching for evidence that will definitively link Harlow to the half-dozen murders. They are seeking poisons, trophies, and, most of all, a diary or journal that might explain the madness behind the mask of beauty. "What drives a woman of her poise and breeding to such depravity... is a question we may never answer," Chief Brody added, addressing reporters on the lawn. "The scene inside suggests a tragic end, but whether it was a case of her own twisted games finally catching up to her, or an accidental overdose, remains to be seen." As dusk falls on Codepen City and children in costume prepare to take to the streets, the shadow that has long loomed over this holiday has finally lifted. But for the residents of Elmview Avenue, the memory of the beautiful woman in the tidy house at number 214 will remain a chilling reminder that true monsters often wear the most convincing masks.
